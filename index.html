Wastewater Reuse & Microplastic Removal

M.Tech Thesis (2025 - 2026) Supervised by: Dr. Abhishek Suresh Dhoble

Wastewater Reuse Potential in Agriculture & Biochar for Microplastic Removal

My dual-faceted Master's thesis combines spatial water resource modeling with advanced physical separation engineering to develop a circular economy treatment loop.

Key Research Contributions:

  • UP Wastewater Reuse Case Study: Analyzed rainfall trends, population dynamics, and sewage treatment capacity up to 2050. Proved that current groundwater irrigation is unsustainable and mapped spatial distribution of water stress to identify treated wastewater reuse hotspots.
  • Agro-Waste Biochar Synthesis: Carbonized banana peel, rice straw, and coconut husk using slow pyrolysis. Characterized materials using bulk density, pH, moisture analysis, and FTIR Spectroscopy.
  • Fixed-Bed Column Filtration: Designed and conducted continuous-flow filtration experiments to remove Polyethylene Microplastics (100–200 µm) from irrigation waters.
  • Key Discovery: Physical mixtures of agricultural biochars outperformed co-pyrolysed biochar. Rice straw biochar demonstrated the highest operational stability.
